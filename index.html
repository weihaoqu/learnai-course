What is the purpose of this study?

The purpose of this study is to understand how high school students learn to build real-world software projects when working with AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Claude, and GitHub Copilot) and undergraduate mentors. We want to measure how students' software engineering knowledge and attitudes toward AI change before and after a hands-on AI training session. The goal is to improve how AI learning programs are designed for students in the future.

What will you be asked to do?

You are participating in a single in-person group session at Monmouth University. During this session, you will:

  1. Complete a short knowledge pre-test (~5 minutes) — you already did this.
  2. Watch a live demonstration and work through hands-on AI projects with guidance from Prof. Qu and undergraduate mentors.
  3. Complete a short knowledge post-test after the demonstration (~5 minutes).
  4. Complete a post-session attitude survey via Google Forms (~5 minutes).

You also completed a pre-session attitude survey at home before today's session.

All assessments and surveys cover general software engineering skills and attitudes toward AI. There are no sensitive, personal, medical, or emotional questions.

Will you be recorded?

No. You will NOT be audio- or video-recorded at any point during this study.

Risks and Benefits

RISKS: There are no foreseeable risks to you beyond those experienced in daily life.

BENEFITS: There is no direct benefit to you. The study will indirectly benefit the scientific field by adding new information about how students learn with AI tools.

Confidentiality

Your data will be collected confidentially. Your name is collected only to match your before/after responses (attitude surveys and knowledge assessments). After matching, your name will be replaced with a sequential ID number and the name-ID mapping will be permanently deleted. Data is stored on password-protected Monmouth University institutional systems and retained for 3 years after study completion. No identifying information (name, school, project name) will appear in any publication.
