What Is AI, Really?

Let's watch a 9-minute video, then discuss

Before we watch...

Forget the movies. AI is not a robot. AI is not conscious. AI does not "think." What it actually does is much simpler — and much more useful once you understand it.

Watch for this:

AI is a pattern-matching machine trained on billions of examples. It predicts what comes next based on patterns. That's it. No magic.

5

AI = Prediction, Not Thinking

What AI actually does:

"The cat sat on the ___" AI calculates probabilities: mat → 18% floor → 12% table → 9% ground → 8% Picks the most likely: "mat" Then predicts the NEXT word... and the next... and the next.

What this means for you:

  • AI sounds confident even when it's wrong
  • It doesn't "know" facts — it predicts likely text
  • The same question can give different answers each time
  • You must verify everything it tells you

Key takeaway

AI is autocomplete on steroids. Incredibly useful, but never blindly trustworthy.

The Vibe Learning Trap

The #1 mistake people make with AI

❌ Crutch Mode

"Write my essay."
"Solve this for me."
"Give me the answer."

Result: You submit AI's work. You learn nothing. Next test, you're lost.

vs
✅ Amplifier Mode

"Explain this concept."
"Check my reasoning."
"Quiz me on this."

Result: You do the thinking. AI helps you understand deeper.

The Rule

Own the thinking. Delegate the drafting. If you can't explain what AI gave you, you haven't learned anything.

The 4-Part Prompt Structure

The secret to getting good results from AI

1. CONTEXT — Who you are, what the situation is
2. INSTRUCTION — What you want AI to do, step by step
3. INPUT — The text, data, or question to work with
4. OUTPUT FORMAT — How you want the answer: bullets, table, essay, code
CONTEXT: "You are a debate coach." INSTRUCTION: "Write a persuasive paragraph." INPUT: "Topic: school start times" OUTPUT FORMAT: "200 words, include 2 stats, aimed at a principal"
8

Bad Prompt vs. Good Prompt

Same AI. Completely different results.

Bad prompt
write an essay

Generic. Vague. AI has no idea what you want. You'll get garbage.

vs
Good prompt
You are a high school debate coach. Write a 200-word persuasive paragraph arguing that school should start at 9 AM. Include 2 statistics. Tone: convincing a principal.

Specific context, clear instruction, defined output. Night and day.

The only thing that changed was YOUR prompt

Vague in = vague out. Specific in = useful out. You control the quality.

What Is Claude Code?

Your AI-powered building partner

Claude Code lives in your terminal. You describe what you want to build, and it:

  • Reads your project files
  • Makes a plan
  • Writes the code
  • Runs tests to check its own work
  • Fixes bugs when things break

Think of it like...

A very fast colleague who you talk to by typing messages. You describe what you want, it figures out how to do it.

The Golden Workflow

How to work with AI without losing control

🔎 EXPLORE → 📝 PLAN → ✅ CONFIRM → 💻 BUILD "Look at "Make a "Show me "OK, now my project plan for before you code it" first" this" start"

Never skip PLAN and CONFIRM

If you just say "do it" without planning, AI will build the wrong thing fast. Speed without direction is just fast chaos.

The magic phrase:

"Before you write any code, brainstorm ideas, make a plan, and show me for approval."

11

Always Check AI's Work

AI sounds confident even when it's completely wrong

AI can:

  • 📖 Invent fake citations
  • 🔢 Make up statistics
  • 📅 Present outdated info as current
  • 💬 Say wrong things with total confidence

The Verification Checklist:

  1. Can I find this source?
  2. Does the number make sense?
  3. Is this current?
  4. Does another source agree?
  5. Would my teacher accept this?

Real example

AI said: "According to Smith et al. (2024), 78% of students use AI daily."

Reality: That paper doesn't exist. AI hallucinated the entire citation.

Rule of thumb

If you can't verify it, don't include it. If you can't explain it, you don't understand it.

5 Safety Rules

Memorize these. They'll save you.

🔒 Never paste personal info — no addresses, phone numbers, passwords, private photos
🔍 Never trust without verifying — check every citation, fact, and claim
🚫 Never generate harmful content — no fake images of real people, no misinformation
📚 Know your school's AI policy — every school has different rules. Know yours.
✅ Credit AI when required — if your school requires disclosure, always be honest

Tips for Project Time

If you're stuck:

  • Write down what you want BEFORE typing a prompt
  • Use the 4-part structure: Context, Instruction, Input, Output
  • If AI gives bad output, don't give up — improve your prompt
  • Raise your hand — Prof. Qu is your prompt coach today

Remember

Your first prompt is a draft. Refine it. The best results come from iteration, not one perfect question.

The workflow:

1. Think: What do I want? ↓ 2. Write: Specific prompt ↓ 3. Read: Check AI's output ↓ 4. Fix: Improve the prompt ↓ 5. Repeat until good ↓ 6. Show parents! 🎉
