Big picture

What Is AI, Really?

Let's watch a 9-minute video, then discuss

Before we watch

Forget the movies. AI is not a robot. AI is not conscious. AI does not "think." What it actually does is much simpler — and much more useful once you understand it.

Watch for this

AI is a pattern-matching machine trained on billions of examples. It predicts what comes next based on patterns. That's it.

Core concept

AI = Prediction, Not Thinking

What AI actually does

"The cat sat on the ___" AI calculates probabilities: mat → 18% floor → 12% table → 9% ground → 8% Picks most likely: "mat" Then predicts the NEXT word...

What this means for you

  • AI sounds confident even when wrong
  • It doesn't "know" facts — it predicts likely text
  • Same question → different answers each time
  • You must verify everything

Takeaway

Autocomplete on steroids. Useful, but never blindly trustworthy.

Warning

The Vibe Learning Trap

The #1 mistake people make with AI

Crutch mode

"Write my essay."
"Solve this for me."
"Give me the answer."

You submit AI's work. You learn nothing.

vs
Amplifier mode

"Explain this concept."
"Check my reasoning."
"Quiz me on this."

You do the thinking. AI helps you go deeper.

The rule

Own the thinking. Delegate the drafting.

Prompt engineering

The 4-Part Prompt Structure

The secret to getting good results from AI

1. Context — Who you are, what the situation is
2. Instruction — What you want AI to do
3. Input — The text, data, or question
4. Output Format — Bullets, table, essay, code
CONTEXT: "You are a debate coach." INSTRUCTION: "Write a persuasive paragraph." INPUT: "Topic: school start times" OUTPUT: "200 words, 2 stats, aimed at a principal"
Live demo

Bad Prompt vs. Good Prompt

Same AI. Completely different results.

Bad
write an essay

Generic. Vague. You'll get garbage.

vs
Good
You are a high school debate coach. Write a 200-word persuasive paragraph arguing school should start at 9 AM. Include 2 statistics. Tone: convincing a principal.

Specific. Clear. Night and day.

Your tool

What Is Claude Code?

Your AI-powered building partner

Claude Code lives in your terminal. You describe what you want, it:

  • Reads your project files
  • Makes a plan
  • Writes the code
  • Checks its own work
  • Fixes bugs

Think of it like

A very fast colleague you talk to by typing. You describe what you want, it figures out how.

You: Build me a quiz app about space facts with a scoring system. Claude: I'll create an HTML file with 10 questions, a score tracker, and a results screen... [writes code] [tests it] [opens in browser]
Process

The Golden Workflow

How to work with AI without losing control

🔎 EXPLORE → 📝 PLAN → ✅ CONFIRM → 💻 BUILD "Look at "Make a "Show me "OK, now my project plan" first" build it" first"

Never skip plan and confirm

If you just say "do it" without planning, AI will build the wrong thing fast.

The magic phrase

"Before you write any code, brainstorm ideas, make a plan, and show me for approval."

Critical skill

Always Check AI's Work

AI can

  • Invent fake citations
  • Make up statistics
  • Present outdated info as current
  • Say wrong things confidently

Verification checklist

  1. Can I find this source?
  2. Does the number make sense?
  3. Is this current?
  4. Does another source agree?
  5. Would my teacher accept this?

Real example

AI said: "According to Smith et al. (2024), 78% of students use AI daily."

That paper doesn't exist. AI hallucinated the entire citation.

Rule

If you can't verify it, don't include it.

Non-negotiable

5 Safety Rules

Memorize these

1. Never paste personal info — no addresses, passwords, private photos
2. Never trust without verifying — check every citation, fact, and claim
3. Never generate harmful content — no fake images of real people
4. Know your school's AI policy — every school has different rules
5. Credit AI when required — always be honest about AI assistance

Tips for Building

If you're stuck

  • Write what you want on paper first
  • Use the 4-part structure
  • Bad output? Improve your prompt
  • Raise your hand — Prof. Qu is your prompt coach

Remember

Your first prompt is a draft. The best results come from iteration.

1. Think: What do I want? ↓ 2. Write: Specific prompt ↓ 3. Read: Check AI's output ↓ 4. Fix: Improve the prompt ↓ 5. Repeat until good ↓ 6. Show parents! 🎉
