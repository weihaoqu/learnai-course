# Demo Script
## What to show on the projector and what to say

---

### Opening (0:00 — 3 min)

**Show:** Nothing yet — face the students.

**Say:**
> "Hi everyone! I'm Prof. Qu. Welcome to Monmouth University. Today you're going to learn what AI actually is — not the movie version, the real version. And you're going to BUILD something with it in 30 minutes.
>
> Parents, you're welcome to watch the whole session. At the end, your child will show you what they built.
>
> No coding experience needed. If you can type, you can do this. Let's start."

---

### Pre-Test (0:03 — 5 min)

**Show:** Pre-test link on projector.

**Say:**
> "First, a quick 5-minute quiz. Don't worry — there are no wrong answers. This just helps us see what you already know. Open this link on your laptop."

*Wait for all 5 to finish.*

---

### Consent Form (0:09 — 5 min)

**Show:** https://weihaoqu.github.io/learnai-course/ on projector.

**Say:**
> "Now open this website on your laptop. You'll see a consent form. This explains what we're doing today and how your information is protected. Read it carefully.
>
> If you agree to participate in the research, click 'I Agree.' If you'd rather not, click 'I Do Not Agree' — you can still do all the fun stuff, we just won't use your data for research.
>
> Take your time. Ask me if anything is unclear."

*Wait for all students to click through.*

---

### "What is AI?" Video (0:14 — 10 min)

**Show:** Scroll to "New to AI? Start Here" video on the web app. Play full screen.

**Say (before playing):**
> "This 9-minute video explains what AI is better than I can. Watch closely — I'll ask you what surprised you after."

*Play the Google AI for Beginners video (9:18).*

**Say (after):**
> "So — what surprised you? ... Right. AI is basically a pattern-matching machine trained on billions of examples. It doesn't think. It doesn't know things. It predicts what comes next based on patterns. That's it."

---

### The Vibe Learning Trap (0:26 — 6 min)

**Show:** Navigate to Course 4 → Track B (Think Critically) → Module 0 (Vibe Trap). Play the embedded video (4:38).

**Say (before playing):**
> "Now here's the most important lesson today. This video is about what happens when people use AI without understanding what it gives them. Watch for the word 'vibe coding.'"

**Say (after):**
> "The same thing happens with essays, homework, projects — if AI writes it and you can't explain it, you didn't learn anything. Today we're going to do the OPPOSITE. You're going to understand what you build."

---

### Prompt Structure (0:32 — 3 min)

**Show:** Navigate to Course 3 → Track 1 → Prompt Builder module. Scroll to the Cheat Sheet section.

**Say:**
> "Here's the secret to getting good results from AI. Every good prompt has 4 parts:
> 1. CONTEXT — who you are, what the situation is
> 2. INSTRUCTION — what you want AI to do, step by step
> 3. INPUT — the actual text or data
> 4. OUTPUT FORMAT — how you want the answer: bullet points, essay, code, table
>
> Write vague prompts, get vague answers. Write specific prompts, get useful answers."

---

### Live Demo — Bad vs. Good Prompt (0:35 — 3 min)

**Show:** Open ChatGPT on projector.

**Bad prompt — type:**
> "write an essay"

**Show the generic result. Then say:**
> "See? Useless. Now watch this."

**Good prompt — type:**
> "You are a high school debate coach. Write a 200-word persuasive paragraph arguing that school should start at 9 AM instead of 7:30 AM. Include 2 statistics. Use a tone that would convince a school principal."

**Show the result. Say:**
> "See the difference? Same AI. Completely different result. The only thing that changed was YOUR prompt. That's why prompt structure matters."

---

### Project Time Introduction (0:38 — 2 min)

**Show:** Hold up the project brief cards.

**Say:**
> "Now it's YOUR turn. You have 30 minutes to build something real with AI. Here are 5 projects — pick the one that sounds most fun to you. At the end, you'll show your parents what you made.
>
> If you get stuck, raise your hand. I'll help you write a better prompt. That's my job — I'm your prompt coach today."

*Hand out project briefs. Let students pick.*

---

### During Project Time (0:40 — 30 min)

**Show:** Nothing on projector — focus on students.

**Do:**
- Walk around every 3-5 minutes
- Ask each student: "What are you building? Show me your last prompt."
- If stuck: "What are you trying to get AI to do? Let's write that down on paper first."
- If finished early: "Try improving it. Can you make the output better with a more specific prompt?"
- Praise effort, not just results

---

### Show & Tell (1:10 — 10 min)

**Show:** Each student's screen via projector (HDMI or screen share).

**Say:**
> "OK! Let's see what everyone built. Each person gets 2 minutes. Show us what you made, and tell us one thing you learned."

*Go around. After each: brief applause, one positive comment.*

**After all 5:**
> "You just built real things with AI in 30 minutes. That's agentic engineering — you directed the AI, you made the decisions, you owned the result."

---

### Post-Test + Survey (1:20 — 10 min)

**Show:** Post-test link + survey link on projector.

**Say:**
> "Last thing — same quiz as the beginning, plus a short survey about today. Takes about 10 minutes. Be honest — your answers help us make this better for future students."

---

### Wrap Up (1:30 — 5 min)

**Show:** Web app landing page.

**Say:**
> "Thank you all for coming. Three things to remember:
> 1. AI is a tool — you are the thinker.
> 2. Good prompts beat good AI models every time.
> 3. The web app is yours — explore the other courses at home.
>
> Parents — here's an info sheet about what your child did today. The web app link is on it. If you have any questions, my email is on the sheet."

*Hand out parent info sheets. Thank everyone.*
