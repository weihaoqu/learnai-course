## Handout B: Project Brief Cards (print 1 of each — 5 total)

### Project 1: My AI Study Buddy
**What you'll build:** A personal AI tutor that quizzes you on any subject
**Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude
**Steps:**
1. Pick a subject (history, biology, math — anything you're studying)
2. Type this prompt:
   > "I'm studying [your topic]. Don't give me answers directly. Ask me guiding questions that help me figure it out. Start simple, get harder. Give hints if I'm stuck — never give answers."
3. Have a 10-minute tutoring conversation
4. Screenshot your best exchange
5. Open Course 4 → Track A → Module 4 and export your study system
**What you'll show parents:** Your exported study system + screenshot of AI tutoring you

---

### Project 2: Design a Playable Game
**What you'll build:** A browser game designed with AI
**Tool:** Claude Code (in Terminal)
**Steps:**
1. Think of a game idea: genre, character, goal, obstacle, twist
2. Open Terminal and type `claude`
3. Tell Claude Code:
   > "Build me a simple browser game. It's a [genre] game where [character] tries to [goal]. The main obstacle is [obstacle]. The twist is [twist]. Make it as a single HTML file I can open in my browser. Include a scoring system."
4. Open the HTML file Claude creates — play your game!
5. Ask Claude to improve it: add colors, sound effects, a start screen
**What you'll show parents:** A working game running in your browser

---

### Project 3: 60-Second Explainer Script
**What you'll build:** A complete script for a short explainer video
**Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude
**Steps:**
1. Pick a topic you know well or find interesting
2. Type this prompt:
   > "Write a 60-second explainer video script about [topic]. Structure: Hook (3 sec attention grab), Main point (explain the concept simply with an analogy), Call to action (what the viewer should do next). Target audience: other high school students. Tone: energetic and fun."
3. Read the script out loud — does it sound natural?
4. Ask AI to revise: "Make the hook more dramatic" or "Add a surprising fact"
5. Ask AI to generate image descriptions for each scene
**What you'll show parents:** Your polished script + scene descriptions

---

### Project 4: AI Myth Buster
**What you'll build:** A fact-checked report debunking AI myths
**Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude
**Steps:**
1. Ask AI: "Tell me 5 impressive-sounding facts about AI with statistics"
2. For each fact, use the Verification Checklist:
   - Can I find this source? Search for it.
   - Does the number make sense?
   - Is it current?
   - Would my teacher accept this?
3. Catch at least 1 hallucination (fake fact, made-up citation)
4. Write a short "Myth vs. Reality" for the 3 most interesting claims
**What you'll show parents:** Your "3 AI Myths Busted" report with evidence of what AI got wrong

---

### Project 5: Story Bible + First Chapter
**What you'll build:** A creative story with world-building and an AI-written opening
**Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude
**Steps:**
1. Create your Story Bible:
   - Main character: name + one-line description
   - What they WANT (external goal)
   - What they FEAR (internal conflict)
   - Setting + one world rule that cannot be broken
2. Paste your bible into AI with this prompt:
   > "Here's my story bible. Write the first 500 words of this story. Follow my world rules exactly. Do not break my unbreakable rule. I am the author — you are my writing assistant."
3. Read what AI wrote. Does it follow your rules? Fix anything that breaks them.
4. That editing process — THAT's what being an author means.
**What you'll show parents:** Your story bible + edited first chapter
